EUROPE AND CENTRAL ASIA

UNDP’s work in Europe and Central Asia covers 18 middle-income countries and territories from Southeast Europe to Central Asia.

While this region compares well with middle-income countries in other parts of the world, it also faces a number of important hurdles. Its recovery from the 2008-2009 global financial crisis has been the weakest in the developing world, making social exclusion worse in the labor market and other areas. Its economies are vulnerable to instability in Europe and Russia, as well as to price fluctuations in the global economy. The region also faces large migration flows and high disaster risks, and it is at the center of renewed East-West tensions. Together, these trends are threatening to reverse the region’s development gains.

UNDP is working with a wide range of partners to help countries in Europe and Central Asia reach the Sustainable Development Goals, or SDGs. Our 2016 Regional Human Development Report will assess the region’s inequalities and its prospects for achieving a development breakthrough.
